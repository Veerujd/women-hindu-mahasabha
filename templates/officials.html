LEADERS OF ALL INDIA VEERASHAIVA LINGAYATHA MAHASABHA

Guiding the Akhila Bharata Veerashaiva Lingayatha Mahasabha with vision and devotion.

NATIONAL EXECUTIVE LEADERSHIP

President

Dr. Shamanur Shivashankarappa

Ex-Minister & MLA

Dr. Shamanur Shivashankarappa is a visionary leader who has transformed the infrastructure of the Veerashaiva community. Under his guidance, the Mahasabha has achieved unprecedented growth in educational and medical sectors. His commitment to social welfare has fostered numerous high-impact institutions serving the community across Karnataka. As an industrialist and seasoned statesman, he brings a unique blend of strategic vision and compassionate governance to the organization. His lifelong mission continues to be the preservation of our spiritual core while embracing technological advancement. He has been a stalwart in promoting Vachana Sahitya among the youth, ensuring that the timeless wisdom of Basavanna remains a guiding light for future generations. His influence extends beyond the Mahasabha, shaping the cultural and political landscape of the state with integrity and devotion.

Secretary General

Shri Eshwar Khandre

Cabinet Minister, Govt. of Karnataka

Shri Eshwar Khandre provides dynamic administrative leadership to the Mahasabha's global network. Known for his commitment to social justice, he has spearheaded numerous initiatives aimed at youth empowerment and rural development. His acumen ensures that the organization operates with transparency and efficiency to foster community growth. Driven by a deep passion for service, he effectively bridges the gap between traditional heritage and modern aspirations. His leadership is pivotal in maintaining the administrative excellence and unity of the Mahasabha in a global context. He is a strong advocate for the digital transformation of the organization, aiming to connect every community member through a centralized digital platform. His focus on inclusive growth ensures that the benefits of the Mahasabha's initiatives reach the most remote corners of the community.
