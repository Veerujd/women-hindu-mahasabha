Former Presidents

Honoring the visionary leaders who shaped the Akhila Bharata Veerashaiva Mahasabha since 1904.

1904 - 1906

Late Shri Sirasangi Lingaraj

Foundation visionary who established the Mahasabha's core principles and early unity.

1906 - 1907

Late Sir K.P puttanna Chetty

Distinguished administrator who modernized early organizational structures.

1907 - 1909

Late Shri Rajalakhamana Gowda

Champion of cultural heritage and regional institutional growth.

1909 - 1910

Late Shri Warad Mallappa

Promoted social welfare and spiritual inclusivity within the community.

1910 - 1911

Late Shri Mamale Desai

Strategist who strengthened the Mahasabha during its formative years.

1911 - 1917

Late Shri Artal Rudragowda

Educational reformer focused on youth empowerment and academic initiatives.

1917 - 1918

Late Sir K.P puttanna Chetty

Returned for a second tenure to guide through post-war reorganization.

1918 - 1921

Late Shri Shanthaveerappa Menasinakai

Led early political representation efforts for the Veerashaiva community.

1921 - 1923

Late Shri Siddappa Kambli

Architect of legislative progress for the community in Mumbai-Karnataka.

1923 - 1925

Late Dr. Fakirappa Gurubasappa Halakatti

Renowned scholar and the "Father of Vachana Literature" preservation.

1925 - 1927

Late Shri Siddappa Kambli

Consolidated organizational influence in educational and legal domains.

1927 - 1930

Late Shri S.S. Basavanal

Literary giant who institutionalized scholarly pursuit within the Mahasabha.

1930 - 1933

Late Shri M.S. Sardar

Integrated grassroots social work with the Mahasabha's central mission.

1933 - 1940

Late Shri C.C. Hulkoti

Extended organizational reach into rural districts with a focus on agriculture.

1940 - 1946

Late His Holiness Banthanal Sanganabasava Swamigalu

Spiritual leader who focused on value-based education and orphanage support.

1946 - 1960

Late Dr. D. C. Pavate

Academic luminary who oversaw the formation of unified Karnataka initiatives.

1960 - 1969

Late His Holiness Banthanal Sanganabasava Swamigalu

Returned to lead during a critical phase of post-independence community building.

1969 - 1975

Late Shri J.B Mallaradhya

Focused on administrative excellence and legal protection of institutional assets.

1975 - 1980

Late Shri K.M. Nanjappa

Pioneered social justice initiatives and youth employment programs.

1980 - 1985

Late Shri Iswar Mallappa Magdum

Technocratic approach to community management and engineering projects.

1985 - 1999

Poojya Dr. Sharanabasavappa Appa

Spiritual colossus who expanded the Mahasabha into an international cultural force.

1999 - 2012

Dr. Bheemanna Khandre

Modernized the membership system and integrated the community globally.

2012 - Present

Dr. Shamanur Shivashankarappa

Current Visionary: Championing industry, health, and academic excellence in the new millennium.

The Mahasabha has been honored by the leadership of over 86 distinguished individuals over its 120-year history.
